# Operating Capital Diagnostic
**How to Read Your Team's Answers**

A pre-interview brief for the CEO / operator running an assessment round with their leadership team.

---

This assessment is not a process-quality scorecard. It's a **capital allocation tool for management attention.** Every gap between what your team rates as *important* and how they rate their *competency* is a place where the business is leaving money or time on the table — and where your next hour of leadership attention can earn the highest return.

Read the radar chart with one question: *Where is the gap between "matters most" and "we do this well" largest?* That's where to point your team this quarter.

---

## The four outcome lenses

When you review the gaps, classify each one into one of four buckets. Practices that hit two or more buckets are your highest-leverage moves.

| Lens | The implicit question |
|---|---|
| **Makes money** | If we close this gap, what new revenue opens up? |
| **Saves money** | If we close this gap, what costs disappear? |
| **Saves time** | If we close this gap, what hours come back to leadership? |
| **Preserves money or time** | If we *don't* close this gap, what gets destroyed? |

---

## Five conversations to have with your team — beyond the assessment

The assessment captures what they think; these probe whether they operate like it.

1. **Pricing power.** *When was the last time we raised prices, and what happened?*
   Reveals revenue leak vs commercial confidence.
2. **Unit-economics literacy.** *Without looking it up, what's our blended CAC and customer LTV?*
   Reveals whether the team thinks like operators or like an inbox.
3. **WIP discipline.** *How many strategic initiatives are in flight right now?*
   More than nine = scattered effort = quiet money loss.
4. **Forecast accuracy.** *Last quarter's pipeline forecast vs. actual — how close were we?*
   Reveals sales discipline and capital-planning credibility.
5. **Performance honesty.** *When did you last move on from a B-player who was blocking an A-player?*
   Reveals whether the cost of underperformance is being paid by the company or addressed by the leader.

---

## How this assessment differs from what you've seen before

Most operating diagnostics tell you what's broken. This one is built to tell you **which five to nine things to fix this quarter, ranked by dollar return and speed-to-impact, with evidence required for each upgrade.** The radar chart you'll see today is the starting line. In the next short sprint, the same answers from your team will drive:

- A **ranked priority list** scored by P&L impact × speed × dependency × risk × your company's stage
- A **focus portfolio** with quarterly WIP limits matched to your lifecycle stage (startup, growth, scale, mature)
- An **evidence track** so every claimed upgrade is verified — credible enough to bring to your board or to LPs

The assessment is the diagnosis. The system on top of it is the treatment plan.

---

## What to do during the session

1. Send each leader the share-code link. Give them 30–45 minutes, alone, no Slack.
2. Don't review answers individually first. Look at the **consolidated radar** with the full team in the room — that's where the conversation lives.
3. Pick the **two largest gaps** and run the relevant probe questions above. Take notes.
4. End with one decision: *Of the gaps we've discussed, which two will we commit to closing this quarter?* Write it on a whiteboard.

That single answer, captured as commitment in front of the team, is worth more than any deck or PDF the assessment generates.
